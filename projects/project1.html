Background

The Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia hosts three major industrial cities — D1, D2, and D3 — each developed in different eras and with different industrial profiles. This capstone research investigated their spatial development from 2000 to 2025 using a custom-built geodatabase of 120 factory records across nine industry classification types.

Analytical Methods

01
Kernel Density Estimation
Spatiotemporal density patterns across four time periods.
02
Density per Area
Normalized industrial density relative to city footprint.
03
Location Quotient
Industrial specialization index per city per sector.
04
Near Tool
Proximity of factories to key infrastructure nodes.
05
Buffer Analysis
Influence zones around attraction and repulsion factors.
06
Weighted Overlay
Multi-criteria suitability modeling for industrial location.
07
Sensitivity Analysis
Testing the robustness of weighted overlay results.

Key Finding

Contrary to the assumption that the three cities compete for the same industrial tenants, the analysis revealed a complementary ecosystem — each city developed a distinct industrial identity over time, with D1, D2, and D3 specializing in different industry clusters. This finding has direct implications for future industrial planning and investment allocation in the region.
